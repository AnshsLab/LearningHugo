## The tutorial trap

Most people start with tutorials. I did too. The problem is you can follow along perfectly and still not know how to build anything on your own.

## What changed

The switch happened when I started working on real, small projects — things I actually wanted to exist. Broken code that I had to fix myself. Features I had to research and figure out.

## Key things that helped

1. **Git from day one** — version control forced me to think in structure
2. **Reading docs** — not just Stack Overflow
3. **Building in public** — even just on GitHub

## Still learning

Always will be. That's kind of the point.
